How the Warriors Create Threes: Pace, Space, and Split Action

Golden State’s spacing rules start with pace. Hit ahead, drag, or pitch to the wing and let the slot fill. When the ball finds the elbow, the split-cut menu opens: screen the screener, slip the second action, and force two to the ball.

The magic is in timing. Corners stay occupied, dunker spot lifts late, and the screen angles change with personnel. The result is a steady diet of open catch-and-shoots and backcuts when tags arrive a beat late.

Defenses load to Curry, but the counters—flare-slip, 45-cut, and Spain twists—punish overplays. Mix in early drag screens and the paint touches stack up without grinding the clock.
