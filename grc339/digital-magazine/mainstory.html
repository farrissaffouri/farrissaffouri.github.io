How the Warriors Create Threes: Pace, Space, and Split Action

The Warriors generate high-value threes by flowing from early offense into quick-hitting actions. Rim runs and drag screens force help, then the ball finds the open side.

At the elbow, split action layers two cutters and a screener. When defenders top-lock or switch, slip timing creates rim pressure; stay home and the guard gets a clean catch-and-shoot.

Motion principles—pass and chase, exit screens, and GPII corner cuts—keep weak-side defenders occupied. The result is a steady diet of rhythm threes and back-cuts.
